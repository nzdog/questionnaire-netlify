Protocol Reflection Questionnaire

Reflecting on Your First Walk

Purpose

This questionnaire helps you reflect on your experience using a protocol for the first time. Your responses reveal how you naturally engage with structured reflection and what matters most in your decision-making process.

How to Use

Take your time with each question. There are no right or wrong answers — just notice what feels true for you. Even small observations matter.

Question 1

What was it like using a protocol for the first time?

The first experience often reveals how you naturally approach reflection — whether it felt easy, strange, helpful, or surprising. This helps you understand your own rhythm with structured thinking.

Examples:
"I liked having something to guide me — it stopped me overthinking."
"It felt a bit weird talking to AI at first, but I got into it."
"It was calmer than I expected — it made things clearer without forcing a decision."
Question 2

What stood out most from the process?

Highlighting what caught your attention helps surface the real insight or turning point of the session.

Examples:
"I noticed I already knew my answer — I just hadn't said it out loud."
"Seeing the outcomes written down helped me recognise where I actually am."
"I realised how much my energy drops when I ignore my gut."
Question 3

How did the AI feel as a reflection partner?

Your reaction to the AI — whether comfortable, awkward, or surprisingly natural — helps shape how to use it next time.

Examples:
"It was more neutral than a person, which made it easier to be honest."
"It felt a bit robotic at first, but it stayed consistent."
"It actually helped me stay focused instead of spiralling."
Question 4

Did anything shift in your clarity or confidence after completing it?

This question checks whether the process actually moved something — big or small. Even a subtle shift matters.

Examples:
"I'm clearer that I'm not ready to move yet."
"I don't feel stuck anymore — I just need more information."
"I realised the decision doesn't have to be perfect to be right."
Question 5

What would you change or keep the same next time you use a protocol?

Every walk teaches you something about how you like to reflect. Capturing that now improves the next experience.

Examples:
"I'd take more time with the questions instead of rushing through."
"It worked best when I spoke my answers out loud first."
"I'd like to try it on something more personal next time."
Question 6

One sentence summary — what did this experience show you about how you make decisions?

A simple sentence anchors the learning — not about the job, but about how you think and act under uncertainty.

Examples:
"I usually already know the answer; I just need space to hear it."
"I tend to rush decisions when I'm uncomfortable."
"I'm more confident when I slow down and name what matters."

Thank You

Your reflections help us understand how protocols work in practice and what makes them useful. This feedback shapes how we design future experiences.
